History of Jubilee

Since the inception of the celebration in 1963, Jubilee has been an end of year tradition hosted by the Carolina Union Activities Board that usually lasted the entire weekend. As any tradition, it's changed over time. After 1973, Jubilee was no more — until it was brought back in 2015.

1960s

The Four Preps headlined the first ever Jubilee concert, bringng in around 10,000 people for the first ever Jubilee weekend.The Chad Mitchell Trio and Iain Hamilton also performed. Since the first concert, the festival grew, both in numbers of performers and attendees. In 1966, Jubilee hosted Bitter End Singers, David della Rosa, Brooks, Jay and the Americans, Al Hirt and Charlie Byrd, all of whom performed for a total of about 12,700 people.

1970s

Since its origin, the last Jubilee concert was held April 30 to May 2, 1971 with performers including Chuck Berry, Spirit, Cowboy, Muddy Waters, the J. Geils Band, the Allman Brothers, and Tom Rush, among others. Due to growth each year, the weekend concert attendance was 49,500 for Friday, Saturday and Sunday shows.

A week after the festivals were held, Student Activities Group decided Jubilee should be discontinued due to the increasingly rowdy, noisy and reckless behavior exhibited by students. According to The Daily Tar Heel back issues, cries of discontinuation had been heard since at least 1965. The group recommended programming throughout the year instead.

1980s, 1990s, 2000s

The tradition of a spring concert couldn't be stopped. In 1979, the first annual Springfest was held, sponsored by Student Congress and HRC. In 1980, the first annual Chapel Thrill concert was held, large productions with a budget of $150,000 in the 1981-1982 year. For comparison, that was the entire budget of the Student Union at the time. In 1982, the Chapel Thrill concert made more than $23,000 in profit.

But around 1987, Chapel Thrill came to an end after a struggle for financing, followed by the end of Springfest in 1992.

2010s

CUAB announced the return of Jubilee with hip-hop headliners Rae Sremmurd in 2015. With a budget of between $40,000 and $50,000 for the concert, CUAB sold out of 1,800 tickets in approximately four days. The concert was held in Hooker Fields.

Now, for Jubilee 2016, CUAB has spent $77,000 on country musician Chase Rice. The number of tickets avaliable has not been released yet, but the concert will be held at Carmichael Stadium.
